Vanika Hans

A creative space for whatever may unfold.

Welcome. After spending many years of my life in International Baccalaureate (IB) as well as in Engineering School, I've found that although my studies present many technical challenges on a daily basis, finding a creative medium is a challenge in itself. Thus, I have created a compilation of both my technical achievements, as well as my side ambitions, in addition to my thoughts on self-improvement, tech, the world as we know it, and anything else that may come to mind.

I'd like to thank you for visiting this site, and I hope that this method of creativity truly does impact you in one way or another: whether it's inspiration to make your own website, immediate repulsion by my content (if so, please send me your feedback!), or at the very least a break from your extremely busy day.

About

The Technical-Pitch

I am currently a Senior majoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering with a minor in Computer Science and a Leadership Certificate. I currently attend the University of Colorado in the beautiful city of Boulder. In addition to my studies, I am currently a part of the Engineering Honors Program (EHP), the Engineering Leadership Program (ELP), the Esteemed Scholars Program (James H. Baker), and Broadening Opportunities through Leadership and Diversity (BOLD).

Over the course of the past years of school, I have been primarily trained in Embedded Software, Linear Systems, Microelectronics, Digital Logic, Computer Organization/Microarchitecture, Operating Systems, Data Structures, Algorithms, Control Systems, and Digital Signal Processing.

Further, I have also held internships at many different companies. My first internship was at DragonDev, a local Boulder start-up, where I worked as a Junior Developer and helped research, design, and develop business applications while also working on the HTML and CSS for the company's website. Next up, I worked as an IT Intern at Hunter Douglas in Broomfield, Colorado. Here, I was assigned the task to synchonize the company and dealer database using programmatic solutions in C++, iMacro, Unix, and Excel. Most recently, I interned at Intel Corporation in Fort Collins, Colorado. I worked on two Validation projects, the first where I analyzed Isolation Strategies between Power Domains in UPF, and the second where I found errors in RTL Functionality using X-Propogation. Both of these projects were done using DVE and Verdi. I found that I enjoyed this internship so much, and thus I will be working full-time at Intel in Hillsboro, Oregon. Really looking forward to it.

The Non-Technical Pitch

Besides my full-time job as a student, I am extremely involved in the Society of Women Engineers (SWE). I have held leadership positions as the Director of K-12 Outreach, the University of Colorado Engineering Council and Student Learning Center Representative, and (currently) Vice President of the Chapter. Beyond SWE, I am very passionate about Dance; I was previously on the Verve Collisonz Hip-Hop Team at CU, and since then have had the opportunity to perform Bollywood, a dance form blending hip-hop and Indian Culture, and Bharatanatyam, an Indian Classical Dance, on many occasions. I have served as a "Toon Leader," analogous to Learning Assistant, for the Gateway to Leadership Course, a fundamental course as part of ELP, as well as a "Recitation Leader," also analogous to Learning Assistant, for EHP. Both of these positions involved organizing weekly recitations with a group of 6-7 students in order to further discuss concepts, and to allow students a platform to share their thoughts. I have also previously worked as a tutor as part of the Electrical, Computer, and Energy Engineering Department's Knowledge Foundry program. I assisted students here with Circuits 1, Intro to Programming in C and MATLAB, and Digital Logic.

In my spare time, I very much enjoy hiking, photography/film, movies, and learning new things.
